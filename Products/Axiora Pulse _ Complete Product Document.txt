Most early-stage entrepreneurs across India and emerging markets fail not because their ideas are 
bad — but because they cannot prove their idea is good. They build first and ask later. 
Today's founder has only four bad options when they need market proof: 
•        Ask friends and family — biased, useless for investors.

at idea stage.

start-up death.

5.  Product Modules — The Six Engines 
Axiora Pulse is not a si ngle tool. It is a stack of six specialised engines, each solving a different 
decision-making problem. The MVP launches with the first two; the rest follow as the platform 
matures. 
5.1  Module 1 — AI Survey & Idea Validation Engine  (MVP) 
Solves:  "Will people actually buy or use my idea?" 
The flagship engine. User enters an idea, uploads custom questions, or lets the AI generate 
everything. Output: ready-made survey, editable questions, audience targeting, response analytics, 
market validation report. 
Used by:  start -up founders, brands testing products, film producers testing audience interest, 
creators testing content ideas. 
5.2  Module 2 — Polling Dashboard  (MVP) 
Solves:  "What does the majority prefer?" 
Different from surveys — built for fast, quic k decisions. Use cases: election polling, political 
sentiment, college elections, community voting, employee voting, product preference voting 
("which logo should we choose?", "which movie title is better?"). 
Used by:  political campaigns, communities, internal teams, brands. 
5.3  Module 3 — Feedback Dashboard  (V2) 
Solves:  "How can we improve?" 
Continuous-feedback engine for businesses already in market. Customer feedback, product 
reviews, service ratings, employee feedback, event feedback — all in one dashboard. 
Used by:  SMEs, restaurants, clinics, retail chains, education institutions. 
5.4  Module 4 — Market Research Dashboard  (V2) 
Solves:  "What is happening in this market right now?" 
This is where Pulse becomes a true intelligence platform. User enters a market thesis ("I want to 
launch a healthy drink in Hyderabad") and the platform layers survey + poll + competitor insights 
+ demand trends + consumer sentiment into one report. 
Used by:  founders entering new categories, expansion teams, investors evaluating sectors. 
5.5  Module 5 — Voting & Community Decision Dashboard  (V3) 
Solves:  "How do we decide together?" 
Group decision -making for organisations: apartment communities, college committees, startup 
teams, NGO boards, internal task forces. Lightweight, transparent, traceable. 
5.6  Module 6 — Consumer Sentiment Dashboard  (V3) 
Solves:  "How are people reacting to our brand?" 
AI analyses reviews, survey responses, polling data, and social media sentiment  to give brands a 
real-time emotional read on their market. This is the moat layer — the thing that turns Pulse from 
a tool into infrastructure.

6.2  The AI Engine — The Core Differentiator 
The AI layer is what separates Pulse from every legacy survey tool on the market. It runs on a 
GPT-4 class LLM and does five things no competitor does in one product: 
•        Understands idea context — industry, problem space, target audience. 
•        Auto-generates survey questions across five validated research dimensions: Problem 
Awareness, Solution Fit, Willingness to Pay, Usage Frequency, Demographic Profiling. 
•        Scores each completed survey set into a Market Interest Score out of 100. 
•        Generates a plain-English AI summary of the findings — what the data actually means 
for the founder. 
•        Suggests next steps based on outcomes: pivot, proceed, or refine. 
6.3  Smart Engagement Features  (Why Surveys Actually Get 
Filled) 
Most survey tools die at distribution. Axiora Pulse solves this with a layer of behavioural -
intelligence features: 
Auto-Reminders 
If a participant hasn't filled the survey, the system automatically sends reminders at smart intervals 
until completion. 
Visual Share Cards 
Every survey link, when shared on WhatsApp or social, generates a visually appealing thumbnail 
to spark curiosity and drive participation. 
Personalised Conversational Nudges 
Reminders are written in a friendly tone, include contextual ho oks, and are sent at optimal times. 
Three sub-types: 
•        Personalisation — "Hey, your inputs on team challenges were super useful. Can you 
finish the last few questions?" 
•        Smart timing — evenings and lunch breaks, never spam, max 2 –3 reminders, stop 
instantly when complete. 
•        Progress-based — "You're 60% done — just a few more questions!" / "Only 3 
questions left." 
•        Value-based — "Your inputs will shape how we solve real business problems." / 
"We'll share the insights with you once the survey closes." 
DIY Personalised Surveys 
Beyond the AI generator, participants and creators can design their own surveys with guided 
support — turning Pulse into a self-serve research school, not just a tool.
